## 1. PROFILE _(Optional)_

Write a brief summary of your qualifications and experience related to the job posting. It can be point form or a paragraph. Highlight the number of years in certain occupation if applicable. Include personal skills requested in the job posting (e.g. adaptable, innovative, quick learner, positive etc.)

**Additional Section Titles:** Career Profile, Personal Profile, Career Summary, Profile Summary, Professional Profile

---

## 2. PROGRAM-RELATED SKILLS or SUMMARY OF QUALIFICATIONS _(Essential)_

**Additional Section Titles:** Summary of Skills, Skills, Highlights of Qualifications, Skills & Competencies, Industry-Specific Skills

**Guidelines:**

- Include sub-headings if applicable
- Review course outlines/descriptions to identify the industry-related skills gained during studies
- Include both technical skills gained from your program and employability/transferable skills gained throughout your work, volunteer and academic experiences (e.g. interpersonal, communication, organizational, problem solving, leadership, teamwork skills and/or other soft skills)
- List skills in the order they appear on the job posting for each position to reflect the order of importance from the employer's viewpoint
- Avoid including a simple bullet point list of your skills. Include brief examples of how you have obtained or utilized these skills to add weight and validity to this section
- **DO NOT list skills/knowledge that cannot be supported by examples**

---

## 3. EDUCATION _(Essential)_

**Format:**

- Full name of Diplomas/Degrees/Certificates | Start – End Date
- Name of Institution, City, Province or Country (if outside of Canada)

**Guidelines:**

- Include current program at Algonquin College and previous post-secondary education
- List your education in reverse chronological order (most recent credential first)
- Co-op students should hyperlink their program name to the program website
- Include GPA achieved for each if strong (e.g. 3.0/4.0 and above)

---

## 4. ACADEMIC PROJECTS _(Optional)_

**Format:**

- Name of Academic Project/Lab | Start – End Date
- Name of Course/Program, City, Province or Country (if outside of Canada)

**Guidelines:**

- **Note:** If you do not have previous paid work experience, this section is essential to add
- Include 2-3 academic projects related to the job/industry; if you choose to include relevant personal projects as well, use the section title Projects, instead of Academic Projects
- Describe each project by including 4-6 bullets that describe tasks, transferable skills used, and results achieved (Task + Skill + Outcome)
- **Begin each project description bullet with an action verb**
- Include metrics (e.g. numbers, statistics, percentages) to quantify your project descriptions, if applicable
- Start with the tasks/accomplishments most relevant to the duties listed in the job posting

---

## 5. WORK EXPERIENCE _(Essential)_

**Format:**

- Job Title | Start – End Date
- Employer, City, Province or Country

**Guidelines:**

- Include your previous paid work experience. List the job title, employer/organization, dates and location
- **Note:** If you do not have previous paid work experience, it is essential to include a Volunteer/Extra-Curricular Activities section in your resume as well as highlight your practical projects completed during your studies in the Academic Projects section
- Describe each job by including 4-6 bullets that describe tasks, transferable skills used, and results achieved (Task + Skill + Outcome)
- Include metrics (e.g. numbers, statistics, percentages) to quantify your job descriptions
- **Begin each job description bullet with an action verb**

**Note:** Consider the following points before adding metrics to your job descriptions:

- Are your metrics appropriate in the context of your job (e.g. entry level positions do not need metrics)?
- Are your metrics relevant for the job you are applying for?
- Would you be able to explain how you gathered the metrics you are presenting in an interview?

**Additional Section Titles:** Related Experience, Work Experience, Other Experience, Relevant Experience

---
